Contacts and Resources

If you need assistance or more information, please reach out to the following contacts:

  • Emergency Services: If there is immediate danger, call 999.
  • Local authorities or the police: Referrals can be made to either the police, or a local authorities such as a council - all counties have a Prevent safeguarding team. Contacting authorities will not get the reported person in trouble, but will help them get the support they need.
  • Support Hotline: The national police Prevent line can be reached by calling 0800 011 3764 to discuss concerns.
  • Advice: For advice and guidance, visit the ACT Early website.

What happens when I make a referral?

Referring someone to Prevent, or recieving support from them, will not go on a person's record and is not a punishment, neither is a referrel to the Channel programme. If you suspect that someone may be in trouble, report your concerns straight away.
