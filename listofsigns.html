List of Signs

Some signs of a person being radicalised may include:

  • Sudden withdrawal from family and friends
  • Expressing extreme political or religious views
  • Increased secrecy, especially around internet use
  • Changes in appearance and behavior
  • Becoming increasingly argumentative and refusing to listen to different points of view
  • Sympathy for extremist causes
  • Accessing extremist literature and imagery
  • Being unwilling to engage with people they might see as different
  • Justifying the use of violence for societal issues

Reasons for radicalisation

There are a number of reasons why people may end up being radicalised. Some of these may include:

  • Feeling socially isolated
  • Issues with their home lives
  • Experiencing racism or discrimination
  • Low self-esteem
  • Mental health issues
  • Pressure from peers who have been radicalised themselves
  • Difficulty with understanding consequences or empathy

It is factors such as these that extremists prey upon for their recruitment tactics, often offering their victim some sort of outlet (such as a sense of belonging).
